# Why Luna Vault exists

> The living workspace for your brand.

Luna Vault is a brand infrastructure platform that helps designers and organisations organise, document, hand over, and manage brand systems.

Unlike traditional cloud storage or Digital Asset Management systems, Luna Vault is built around a simple belief:

**A logo is a file. A brand is a system.**<br />
**A brand is not a folder.**

Every logo, icon, pattern, template, colour, and guideline should have context, history, ownership, and purpose.

## The problem after delivery

I'm an industrial designer by training.

Over the years I've designed logos, visual identities, merchandise, brands, websites, and products for startups, businesses, student founders, and organisations.

Something always bothered me.

The design process is well understood: research, concepts, iterations, refinement, and delivery. But almost nobody talks about the moment after delivery.

Designers spend weeks or months crafting a thoughtful identity, only to hand over hundreds of exported files inside a Google Drive folder, Dropbox link, or ZIP archive. Months later, everyone is asking the same questions:

> Which logo is the latest?

> Do we have the SVG?

> Which favicon should we use?

> Where's the white version?

> Is this still approved?

Eventually, the designer becomes the human search engine for the brand they created. That shouldn't happen.

A great brand deserves a great home. Luna Vault exists to make brand handover as intentional as the design process itself.

## Our philosophy

Design doesn't end when the logo is approved.

Design continues through organisation, documentation, consistency, future updates, and helping every future employee, developer, marketer, printer, partner, and client confidently use the brand correctly.

Luna Vault isn't replacing the creative process. It's protecting it.

## The Brand owns the truth

Luna Vault is built around one durable product principle:

**A Brand should only be defined once. Everything else references it.**

Each Vault automatically owns a Parent Brand. That Brand and its descendants define Identity through Logo, Colour, and Typography, and own their Assets as documented Brand objects. Guide, Collections, Packages, exports, and future utilities consume that source rather than maintaining duplicate information.

For the MVP, Brand hierarchy stops at Parent Brand, Sub-brand, and Nested Sub-brand. Assets remain scoped to the Brand they belong to, and Guide owns authored documentation rather than identity values.

Folders organise views of Assets; they do not own files. Collections are committed external releases, while Packages are generated technical deliveries for consumers such as web, application, and game teams.

## Vision

We believe every brand should have a living workspace instead of a static folder—one place where teams can:

- understand their identity
- find approved assets
- explore brand guidelines
- manage sub-brands
- organise campaigns
- document decisions
- hand over projects professionally
- keep brands alive long after the original designer has left
